History of Emoticons

The emotion icon, also know as the “emoticon” is a representation of a facial expression, formed by various combinations of keyboard characters, and is used in electronic communications to convey the writer’s feelings or intended tone. The first emoticon [intentionally created] was made by Scott Fahlman, a computer scientist at Carnegie Mellon University. The following is Dr. Fahlman’s message in which the first ‘humble smiley’ was utilized:

19-Sep-82 11:44 Scott E Fahlman :-)

From: Scott E Fahlman

I propose that the following character sequence for joke markers:

:-)

Read it sideways. Actually, it is probably more economical to mark things that are NOT jokes, given current trends. For this, use

:-(

Fahlman proposed a colon, hyphen and bracket as a way of conveying emotional meaning via plain text.It was at this moment in which he paved the way for a reliance on visual cues that is heavily practiced today.

Not to be confused with the well-known “emoji,” emoticons can be created by anyone, using a keyboard, their fingers, and a vivid Imagination. The following are some clever examples of emoticons:

*<|:-) (Santa Claus)

+<|:-) (the Pope)

===========|:-} (Abraham Lincoln)
